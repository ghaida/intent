## Design Context

### Users
Designers and developers who use AI coding tools (Claude Code, Cursor, VS Code Copilot) and want to bring UX rigor to their design decisions. They are practitioners — not browsing casually, but evaluating whether Intent is worth installing. They value depth over flash, substance over marketing. They'll judge the site by whether it feels like it was designed by someone who actually understands design.

### Brand Personality
**Sharp, opinionated, confident.** Takes strong positions. Unapologetic depth. Feels like a senior designer's personal toolkit — not a product trying to sell you something, but a practitioner sharing their methodology.

Tagline: **Design with Intent.**

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Tone:** Bold and minimal. Editorial quality, like a handcrafted personal site (Rauno.me energy). Distinctive typography, considered spacing, nothing decorative without purpose.
- **Theme:** Light and dark mode with toggle. Both modes should feel equally intentional — not one mode that's "real" and one that's an afterthought.
- **Anti-references:** No generic SaaS landing pages (hero/features/pricing/CTA). No dense documentation wikis. No flashy parallax/3D marketing. No minimal-to-empty pages with no character. Bold and minimal — character without excess.
- **Memorable elements:** The anti-pattern catalog (72+ named dark patterns — nothing else has this), the depth of each skill (real methodology, not surface-level prompts), the philosophy (every design decision should have a reason, visible at every layer).

### Visual Design (Locked)
- **Display font:** General Sans 700 (Fontshare)
- **Body font:** Hanken Grotesk (Google Fonts)
- **Color palette:** Deep Indigo
  - Light mode: `#fafafc` bg, `#18182b` fg, `#5a5a70` muted, `#4338ca` accent, `#dc2626` critical, `#b45309` high
  - Dark mode: `#10101c` bg, `#e0e0f0` fg, `#7878a0` muted, `#7c6ff0` accent, `#f06868` critical, `#e0a040` high
  - Neutrals tinted cool (toward indigo hue) in both modes
- **Grid:** 4px base grid, 2px for fine details
- **Structural metaphor:** Blueprint — dot grid background, monospace section labels with icons, margin annotations
- **Layout:** Two-column (180px label | 48px gap | content) on desktop, single column on mobile

### Design Principles
1. **Every element earns its place.** If it doesn't serve a purpose, remove it. Bold means purposeful, not loud.
2. **The site IS the proof.** A UX design system's website must itself be impeccably designed. The site demonstrates Intent's values by embodying them.
3. **Depth on demand.** Surface the philosophy and breadth immediately. Let users drill into individual skills, the anti-pattern catalog, and methodology when they're ready.
4. **Sharp voice.** Short, direct copy. No marketing fluff. The writing style should match the skills' own voice — rigorous, evidence-grounded, opinionated.
5. **Respect the visitor.** No tracking banners, cookie walls, newsletter popups, or engagement tricks. A UX ethics system cannot use dark patterns on its own site.
